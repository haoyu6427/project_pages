A Wearable Platform to Augment Spatial Cognition for People with Blindness and Low Vision

Abstract

Spatial cognition refers to the ability to gain knowledge about surroundings and utilize this information to identify location, acquire resources, and navigate effectively. People with blindness and low vision (pBLV) face significant challenges due to limited visual input. We present ChatMapâ€”a wearable platform that augments spatial cognition through multi-modal foundation models, combining a wearable camera, audio interface, and vision-language models such as GPT-4V. The platform translates visual scenes into proprioceptive, intelligible guidance. With innovations like task-specific prompt engineering and chain-of-thought reasoning, ChatMap enhances scene understanding, object localization, and risk assessment. Real-world experiments and ablation studies validate its effectiveness, demonstrating superior assistive capability for pBLV individuals.
